Questions

The things people ask first.

Will this actually get me more customers?

That's the whole point. A fast site that ranks on Google, plus an assistant that answers and books every lead — even after hours — means fewer missed calls and more booked jobs. I'd rather build you something that pays for itself than something that just looks nice.

Do you really do AI, or is it a buzzword?

Real AI. I've shipped an image-recognition model at 98.8% accuracy and an automated transcription pipeline. For your business that usually means an assistant trained on your services that answers customers and books appointments, an automation that handles intake or follow-up, or an honest audit of where AI actually pays off — and where it doesn't.

I'm not techy — is that going to be a problem?

Not even a little. You never touch code, servers, or settings — I handle all of it, in plain English. If you can send a text, you can run what I build you, and I'm one message away whenever you need a hand.

How much does a website cost?

Launch sites start at $899, and a site that sells or books with an AI chat assistant included starts at $2,499. Legacy and memorial sites start at $499, and improving an existing site (SEO, competitor analysis, AI) starts at $299. Every project gets a fixed price up front — no surprises.

Can you host it cheaply?

Yes. Many of my sites run on GitHub Pages and similar static hosting, which costs $0/month. Usually your only ongoing cost is a domain name — about $12–20 a year.

How long does it take?

A Launch site is usually 2–3 weeks. Larger sites with booking, a shop, or AI run 4–8 weeks. I work fast and finish.

Will you maintain it after launch?

If you want me to — continuous upkeep starts at $39/month (updates, backups, hosting help, and a human who answers), or a one-time $99 when you just need a few changes. Or I'll hand you the keys and a short guide — you own everything either way.

Do you build memorial and legacy websites?

Yes — it's a specialty. I take a lifetime of material (sermons, scholarship, art, family history) and turn it into a searchable, beautiful archive built to outlive the next platform that shuts down.
