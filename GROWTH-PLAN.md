## 0. The one-sentence positioning

> **Werth Design builds fast, beautiful websites and puts AI to work — for local businesses and
> creators who want more customers, and for the keepers of a life's work who want it to last.**

Two engines, one studio:
- **Money engine (volume + easy sell):** small-business websites + AI implementation.
- **Soul engine (differentiation + word-of-mouth):** legacy / memorial / life's-work archives.

The soul engine is *why people trust you with the money engine*. A builder who lovingly preserved a
pastor's 641 sermons and a scholar's life work is obviously going to sweat the details on a
plumber's booking page or a law firm's AI intake bot.

## 1. The correct audience (who we sell to)

### What the market says (June 2026 research)
- **Small-business websites** are steady, evergreen demand: **$2,000–$8,000** per project from a
  freelancer/small studio, advanced builds $15k+. Maintenance $50–$500/mo. Hottest verticals:
  local service businesses, restaurants, salons, medical/dental, law, real estate.
  *(webfx.com, gruffygoat.com, levitate.ai — 2026 pricing guides)*
- **AI implementation for SMBs** is the growth money: **$5,000–$25,000/project**, **$150–$350/hr**,
  retainers **$2,000–$8,000/mo**, fractional "AI officer" same range. Entry AI audits/POCs
  $10k–$25k. Demand is strong and *trust in faceless vendors is low* — a wedge for a local human.
  *(aiessentials.us, groovyweb.co, theaiconsultingnetwork.com — 2026)*
- **Digital legacy** is a real, growing market (**$23B → $47B by 2031, ~13.7% CAGR**, North America
  ~37%). But the commodity end is **$45–$120 templates** (Legacy.com, A Remembered Life, Keeper).
  Don't compete there — own the **premium bespoke** end (a corpus, an archive, a true life's work).
  *(mordorintelligence.com, grandviewresearch.com — 2026)*

### Ideal Client Profiles (ICPs), in priority order

**ICP 1 — Local service business that lives or dies on getting found (PRIMARY, easiest sell).**
Plumbers, electricians, HVAC, landscapers, dentists, chiropractors, attorneys, real-estate agents,
salons, restaurants in Wilmington / Philadelphia / South Jersey. Pain: an outdated or no website,
losing calls to competitors, no time. Buy trigger: a competitor outranks them, or they're sick of
missing leads. **Sell: Launch or Grow package + an AI assistant that answers and books 24/7.**

**ICP 2 — Established small business ready to add AI (PRIMARY, highest margin).**
Already has a site and customers; hears "you should be using AI" everywhere; doesn't trust the hype
or know where to start. Pain: repetitive busywork (intake, scheduling, FAQs, follow-up). Buy
trigger: a busy season, a hire they'd rather not make. **Sell: AI audit → assistant/automation →
care retainer.** This is the recurring-revenue path.

**ICP 3 — Creator / author / artist / small institution selling something (SECONDARY).**
Artists (like Laurie), authors/guide-writers (like the wine compass), course/membership owners
(like the driving school). Pain: a storefront or funnel that actually converts. **Sell: Grow
package with shop/membership + SEO funnel.**

**ICP 4 — Keeper of a life's work / family commissioning a legacy (SIGNATURE, premium bespoke).**
Retiring or late pastors, scholars, professors, craftspeople; families preserving a parent's
sermons, writing, art, photos, or history; churches and small nonprofits with an archive trapped in
a dying format. Pain: a lifetime of material that's scattered, fragile, or about to be lost. Buy
trigger: a retirement, a memorial, a milestone, a website host shutting down. **Sell: Bespoke
legacy archive.** Long sales cycle, deeply emotional, generates referrals and testimonials worth
more than the invoice.

### Where the money and the meaning meet
Lead generation with ICP 1 & 2 (they pay reliably and recur), let ICP 4 be the story that makes
you unforgettable and referable.

  it. Each project links out and tells a one-line outcome story.

### C. Niche, referral-driven channels for ICP 4 (legacy)

  department admins and emeritus faculty about rescuing scholarship from dead formats.

  Loom of *one* concrete improvement (speed, mobile, an AI booking bot), send it. Show, don't tell.

### E. Content that compounds (SEO + authority)
- A few cornerstone articles tuned to ICP search intent (see §3). Quality over volume.
- A short "before/after" or case-study post per project, each targeting a keyword cluster.

- **Open Graph + Twitter cards** for clean social/link sharing.

## 4. Brand & voice

**Brand idea:** *Werth → worth.* Things that are **worth keeping**, built by someone who cares.
Heirloom craft meets modern capability. The visual language is **editorial/archival** (warm paper,
ink, oxblood, brass; Fraunces + Newsreader) — deliberately the *opposite* of purple-gradient AI
slop. That contrast is the point: it signals a human who sweats craft, which is exactly what makes
the AI offering trustworthy.

**Voice principles:**
- **Plainspoken.** Short, declarative sentences. Concrete nouns. No "synergy," "solutions,"
  "leverage," "cutting-edge."
- **Confident, not boastful.** State outcomes and proof; let the work carry it.
- **Warm and personal.** First person ("I'm Matthew"). People buy from a person — especially for
  memorial work and local services.
- **A little literary.** Fits the legacy work; never fussy. ("A lifetime in the pulpit, made
  searchable.")
- **Outcome-led.** Talk about *more calls, getting found, a story preserved* — not features.

**Signature lines:**
- *Websites worth keeping. AI that earns its keep.* (hero)
- *Work worth keeping.* (sign-off / footer)
- *Real AI. Not slideware.* (AI section)

**Don'ts:** stock-photo handshakes, "we" (you're solo — own it), fake urgency, jargon, AI hype.

## 6. Pricing rationale

Tiers shown on the site. Pricing is deliberately **aggressive — set to undercut local agencies and
Wix/Squarespace-plus-a-freelancer quotes and win volume**, then grow accounts via AI add-ons and
care retainers (the recurring revenue is where the margin compounds):

| Package | Price | Why this number |
|---|---|---|
| **Launch** | from **$899** | Aggressive entry — below most local quotes, removes price as an objection for a first project, gets a foot in the door. |
| **Grow** | from **$2,499** | The target sale and "Most chosen" anchor — a converting site **with an AI chat assistant included** (the spearhead, bundled so it's a no-brainer). |
| **Life's work** | from **$499** | Low floor for a simple legacy/memorial site; bigger archives priced to the project. The differentiator, made approachable. |
| **Already have a site?** | from **$299** | Productized entry for the huge "I already have a site" market: SEO + competitor analysis + AI integration, no rebuild. Pairs with the free-audit funnel as the paid next step. |
| **Custom AI** | contact | Assistants/automations/full systems beyond the included chat — quoted per project (market AI builds run $5k–$25k), so no fixed number anchors it low. |
| **Care & updates** | from **$39/mo**, or **$99 one-time** | Recurring revenue + a low-friction one-time option. The compounding part of the business. |

**Strategy:** low entry prices remove the objection and win the first project; **the money is in the
ladder** — free audit → $299 existing-site tune-up → Grow ($2,499 w/ AI) → Custom AI → care retainer.
Lead conversations toward **Grow** (AI included = the differentiator bundled in), and **always offer
the care plan**. Watch margin on Launch/Life's-work at these floors — scope tightly and upsell.
